# Assumptions

The simulation acts as a stylized digital twin focusing on system dynamics rather than a highly granular traffic simulator. The following assumptions are intentionally used to isolate interaction effects, feedback loops, delays and governance dynamics.

---

## 1. Spatial & Travel Assumptions

### Abstract Zones

The environment is divided into a small number of aggregated zones (e.g. center, residential areas, airport) rather than exact geographic coordinates.

The purpose is to model platform-level dynamics rather than realistic city navigation.

---

### Fixed Travel Times

Travel between zones takes a fixed number of time steps defined by a static travel time matrix.

One simulation tick represents approximately five minutes of platform operations. This interval reflects short matching cycles and stylized urban travel times between zones within the platform environment.

Trips within the same zone require a minimum of one tick (approx. 5 minutes).

---

### No Congestion Modeling

The model does not simulate:

- traffic jams
- routing behavior
- road capacities
- congestion spillovers
- dynamic travel speed changes

Travel times remain constant throughout the simulation.

This abstraction isolates instability generated by algorithmic interaction rather than transportation network complexity.

---

## 2. Demand Assumptions

### Stochastic Demand Arrivals

Demand follows a Poisson arrival process:

```text
NewDemand_i(t) ~ Poisson(lambda_i)
```

This represents random short-term ride request generation.

Zone-specific demand intensities are fixed in the baseline model.

---

### Linear Price Response

Demand responds linearly to local prices:

```text
Demand_i(t) = max(0, NewDemand_i(t) - beta * Price_i(t))
```

The model intentionally avoids:

- multinomial logit models
- utility maximization
- heterogeneous customer preferences
- strategic rider behavior

The goal is to isolate the macro-level effects of pricing feedback.

---

### Fixed Destination Patterns

Trip destinations are sampled from a static destination probability matrix.

Destination probabilities do not dynamically adapt over time.

---

## 3. Supply Assumptions

### Fixed Global Fleet

The total number of vehicles in the system remains constant.

Drivers do not:

- enter or leave the platform dynamically
- reject rides
- strategically reposition themselves
- optimize personal profits
- behave strategically as independent economic agents

Vehicles are treated as centrally coordinated operational resources.

---

### Platform Coordination Assumption

The model focuses on platform-level coordination mechanisms rather than decentralized human decision-making.

The main research interest is the interaction between centralized algorithmic control systems operating on shared information signals.

---

### Heuristic Rebalancing

Rebalancing follows a local heuristic:

```text
Score_ki = Deficit_i(t) / Distance_ki
```

The model intentionally avoids:

- global optimization solvers
- integer programming
- optimal transport formulations
- game-theoretic fleet coordination

This keeps the system interpretable and computationally transparent.

---

## 4. Forecasting Assumptions

### Exponential Smoothing

Forecast demand uses simple exponential smoothing:

```text
ForecastDemand_i(t+1) =
    omega * Demand_i(t)
    + (1 - omega) * ForecastDemand_i(t)
```

The model intentionally excludes:

- neural networks
- reinforcement learning
- transformer forecasting
- adaptive machine learning systems

---

## 5. Algorithmic Autonomy Assumptions

### Rule-Based Coordination

"Autonomous" in this model refers to continuous algorithmic decision-making without direct human intervention.

Agents follow predefined update rules rather than learning dynamically.

---

### No Learning Assumption

Agents do not adapt using:

- reinforcement learning
- policy optimization
- self-learning mechanisms
- online parameter updates

All behavioral rules remain fixed during simulation runs.

This abstraction isolates instability emerging from interaction effects, delays and feedback loops rather than model training dynamics.
